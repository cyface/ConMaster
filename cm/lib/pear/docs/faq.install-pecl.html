How do I install a PECL module written in C?

Answer written by Christian Stocker.

The support for C-extensions in PEAR/PECL is not very sophisticated at the moment. Here's a short description of how to get C-modules running on your system until the PEAR::Installer does that for you.

Download the package from PEAR, then untar it somewhere. Go into the newly built directory and do the following steps:

  • phpize

  • ./configure

  • make

  • cp modules/module.so /usr/local/lib/php

The path, where the module.so is expected, can be different on your system (check php.ini and phpinfo()). To actually use the module, you have to add it to the extension directive in php.ini or to load it with dl("module.so") within your php-files.

You can compile this extension directly into your php-binary, too. For this you have to extract the package into php4/ext (in your source directory). After that do also phpize in this new module-directory. But before you can configure your main php-exectuable, you have to do a ./buildconf on the toplevel of the php-sources. Now you can configure/compile your php-executable with the new extension, as you would with all other extensions.
